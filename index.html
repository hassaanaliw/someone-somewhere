    Someone Somewhere, Once World Famous Comedian, Dies at 45

    By Generic InternSEPT. 1, 2017

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story
    • Share
    • Tweet
    • Email
    • More
    • Save

    Someone Somewhere, who following a 1999 best seller went on to write a series of books that at one point was considered to be the gold standard for teaching people comedy, died on Wednesday in his home. He was 45.

    His death was announced in a statement by his publisher.

    In books like “Knock Knock Knockin' on Comedy's Door”, “The Pun Is Within You” and “Who Needs Self-Esteem when you have Humour?”, Mr. Somewhere documented his journey into the comedy world. His work was dominated by the absurdest of characters living in the most preposterous situations, battling problems from curing cancer to Y2K with bad puns, dad jokes. Someone was a big proponent of the thought that "there is literally nothing my humour cannot overcome" and that "comedy was an art form greater than all others".

    His love for the written word transitioned into a short lived, topsy turvey stand up career that hit the highs of his performace at the Apollo in 2003 that was considered to be one of the best in recent years by Normally-Extremely-Mean Critic at The Guardian to the absolute lows of his 2006 US tour that was panned widely, described as "confusing, disturbing and just plain bad" in the Washington Daily.

    A 2006 New York Times Magazine article about him carried the headline “Insanely Funny or Just Insane?”, fuelled by reviews that criticised his comedy for devolving into a mess of barely connected, absurd (more than usual and nowhere near as funny) and over compensating jokes. 2006 marked the end of his public career, with a particularly confounding performance in his hometown of Seattle capping off a bizzare tour.

    The New York Times has collected accounts from various sources who have known or observed Someone throughout his life to help relive his journey. Photo

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Someone's Brother
    Interview in 2001

    To be fair, Someone wasn't really the type of kid I'd ever have expected to become a comedic sensation. He really kept to himself all the time, was never the type to speak out at all. He'd throw in a knock knock joke every now and then during the family dinners but that was the extent of his contribution really. There was one joke he absolutely loved though. It's probably the thing I remember the most about him from back when we were young.

    You: Knock Knock
    Them: Who’s There?
    You: Woo
    Them: Woo Who!
    You: I must be funnier than I thought, you’re getting excited.

    I mean I wouldn't hear from him days at a time and he'd poke his head into my room and just retell that joke. Was always good for a nice chuckle.

    Envy Martin, Someone's Childhood Friend
    Excerpt from his Blog

    It was a meteoric rise for Someone throughout his high school years. Forvever, he'd been the shy kid who talked to no one at all. Like no one. I remember he'd ask me to ask the teachers if he could go and drink water. He was perfectly happy in his own bubble. Then, slowly, throughout middle school he went further and further into his shell, growing more distant. He'd look at the "cool kids" and just run away. Hard to explain really. And suddenly he became this kid with the best humour in the entire grade. Puns were probably the greatest thing in his arsenal. I remember when you could not go a minute without him making an absolutley unecessary play on words. He'd call his Knock Knock Jokes a-door-able. He thought he could knock anyone down with them. I mean, he had everyone in his surrounding area bursting into laughter by the end of his Junior year. The jocks who used to bully him for his money were now rolling around on the floor as he called them soccers for his jokes. The girls he'd always crushed on (very very secretly) were now floored by his comedic sense.

    She: This is pretty corny to be honest.
    He: Yep, it’s absolutely a-maize-ing?

    That was one of his favourite ones. It was his go to when he was meeting a group of people he wasn't really friends with. It always worked.

    To be honest, I have no idea where it came from. It was a complete transformation. I wasn't sure I was talking to the same Someone I'd always known most of the time. The more he got involved with other people with his humour, the more we grew apart.

    The Career Killer, Columnist and Critic at the New York Times
    Excerpt from his review of Someone's final comedy show

    The words I want to use to describe yesterday's disaster of a stand up show aren't ones I'm allowed to publish here. So instead, I'll describe it as a cacophony of barely tolerable puns, borderline offensive anecdotes and self-deprecation beyond the point of reason.

    It seemed to me like that he found himself at a loss of actual comedic content and decided to make a joke out of his own life and comedic career. That new exagerrated laugh that he paraded around for this tour? And those long, dreary anecdotes about the various mishaps that had befallen him thay day? It was everything his writing and standup had made fun of endlessly before. He used to be absurd to make a point, to make a statement. Now, he's just absurd for the sake of it. Unbelievable. What a fall from grace for the man that was responsible for masterpieces like “Knock Knock Knockin' on Comedy's Door”, a book my peers and I consider to be the peak of comedic style within the past decade. Back then, Someone took the cheapest form of comedy and produced jokes like

    Knock Knock
    Who's There
    Cows go
    Cows go who?
    Na, pretty sure they go moo
    or the timeless classic

    What is a social vegan? Someone who avoids meet
    See? Simple and effective. Nothing like what we got yesterday. Yesterday was brash, uninspired, plain unfunny. Everything Someone was not at his peak. I don't know what happened. What happened to the man who was able to do the simple and absurd equally effectively? I feel like he forgot what made him special. He never took himself seriously at the start. His mocking perspective of the comedic world made him funnier than anything else. It didn't ever look like he was trying to be funny. He just was.

    Maybe he felt that the general public had stopped taking interest in his old self? Maybe he thought they wanted something new? Hard to say. All I know is, this Someone isn't the one that had me laughing every time I read his material. Who had me in fits everytime I opened "The Pun is Within You". It's Someone Else. And if he thinks he's winnning over anyone with this new style, he isn't. I know it's a cliche but the crowd yesterday wasn't laughing with him at all.

    They were laughing at him.

    --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Talking to those who knew him, no one saw the events of past Wednesday unfolding. Everyone saw a man who lived to laugh and make others laugh. His colleagues said that they hadn't heard him laugh at jokes (his own usually) as hard as he had been recently, ever before. The general consensus was that he was a fun, loving spirit that loved life and everything to do with it. So, it was that more devestating for the industry when the news broke of his death. No matter how you viewed his recent career path, you have to agree that he, for a significant amount of time, was an absolute comedic genius, one who provided laughter to thousands with his writing and his speech.

    As for why his career and life took the path they took, we can always sit here and speculate. That won't get us anywhere. Hindsight is always 20/20 but no one could have seen this coming from one of the most beloved entertainers in the stand up circuit.

    All we can do is now is honour Someone once more by sitting back, reaching for a copy of "The Pun is Within You" and laughing once more like he always made us.

    We leave you with the very last joke Someone graced us with via a Facebook status from last week.
